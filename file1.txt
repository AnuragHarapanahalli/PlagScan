The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. This is a sample document used for testing the plagiarism detection system. The system uses k-gram hashing combined with a binary search tree to efficiently identify common text patterns between documents. Plagiarism detection is an important tool in academic integrity, helping educators identify when students have copied work without proper attribution. The algorithm works by breaking text into overlapping chunks called k-grams, computing a hash for each chunk, and then comparing the hashes between two documents.
